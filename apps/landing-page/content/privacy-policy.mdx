This notice describes the intended privacy posture for the private BharatFlow AI prototype.

BharatFlow AI is not yet cleared for public commercial launch. Production privacy terms, a data processing agreement, subprocessors list, and customer-facing compliance materials must be reviewed before external availability.

## Prototype Scope

The private prototype may process:

- account and workspace information;
- workflow configuration and test data;
- authentication and security logs;
- uploaded files when file upload workflows are tested;
- analytics events used to improve the prototype; and
- support or feedback shared by internal users.

Do not use real customer personal data, regulated documents, financial records, health records, or production WhatsApp conversations unless the legal, security, and data-processing posture has been approved.

## Workflow Data

Workflow creators are responsible for deciding what information their workflows collect. For DPDP-aware design, every workflow should:

- explain the purpose before collecting data;
- collect only the minimum fields required;
- capture consent for follow-up, marketing, document processing, or WhatsApp outreach;
- avoid sending personal data to AI providers unless approved; and
- provide a human route for privacy or deletion requests.

## AI Providers

AI blocks may send prompts, files, or context to configured providers. Prototype users should avoid sending personal data to AI providers unless they have approval for that provider and use case.

## Security

Secrets must be stored in environment variables or approved secret stores. Credentials, API keys, customer data, and private documents must not be committed to git.
